# Deep Thesis Competitive Analysis — 2026

## Market Map

The VC intelligence space breaks into 5 categories. Deep Thesis touches 3 of them, which is unusual for its price point.

| Category | Players | Deep Thesis? |
|----------|---------|:---:|
| **Data Terminals** (company/deal data) | PitchBook, Crunchbase, Tracxn | Yes |
| **AI Analysis** (scoring, diligence) | CB Insights, Grata, Harmonic | Yes |
| **Startup Discovery** (sourcing) | Harmonic, Landscape.vc, Signal by NFX | Partial |
| **Deal Flow / CRM** | Affinity, Dealum, Edda, Visible.vc | No |
| **Portfolio Monitoring** | Visible.vc, Edda, Fundwave | No |

## Head-to-Head Comparisons

### PitchBook — The Incumbent

- **Price:** $12,000-$70,000/yr per user
- **Data:** 6M companies, 570K investors, 2.7M investments. Analyst-verified, updated hourly.
- **Strengths:** Deepest dataset. Industry standard. Excel plug-ins, LP/fund data, M&A comps.
- **Weakness:** Priced for institutional funds, not angels. No AI-powered analysis — it's a data terminal, not an analyst.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** PitchBook gives you the raw data but makes you do the work. Deep Thesis gives you 8-agent AI analysis, a conversational quant analyst, and scored investor rankings — things PitchBook doesn't attempt. PitchBook wins on data breadth by orders of magnitude. Deep Thesis wins on price (50-100x cheaper) and AI-driven insights. **They serve different buyers.**

### Crunchbase Pro — The Accessible Option

- **Price:** $588/yr (annual) or $99/mo
- **Data:** 4M+ companies. AI-powered natural language search. Heat scores.
- **Strengths:** Affordable. Good for initial company discovery. Simple UI.
- **Weakness:** Shallow depth — funding rounds and basic profiles, but no diligence tools, no scoring rubrics, no analyst agent. Export limits (2K rows/month).
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** Crunchbase is a search engine for companies. Deep Thesis goes deeper — multi-agent analysis, investment memo generation, VC quant agent, investor rankings. Crunchbase is cheaper but gives you surface-level data. **Deep Thesis competes on depth of analysis, not breadth of data.**

### Harmonic.ai — The Sourcing Engine

- **Price:** Not published (enterprise sales)
- **Data:** 30M+ companies, 190M people. Tracks founder movements, hiring signals, stealth-stage companies.
- **Strengths:** Best-in-class for pre-seed/stealth discovery. Founder signal tracking. CRM integrations.
- **Weakness:** Not a CRM, not an analysis tool. Finds companies but doesn't evaluate them. Priced for funds with dedicated sourcing teams.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** Harmonic answers "what should I look at?" Deep Thesis answers "should I invest?" Different parts of the funnel. Harmonic is upstream (sourcing), Deep Thesis is mid-funnel (analysis/diligence). **Complementary, not competitive.**

**How Harmonic sources companies:**
- Foundation is scraping Delaware Secretary of State corporate filings — catches companies at formation before they have a website or press
- Continuous web crawling: LinkedIn profiles, job boards, domain registrations, product launches, company websites, social media, funding announcements
- ML classification layer interprets raw signals (e.g., "stealth company just hired 3 ML engineers and registered a .ai domain") and surfaces them before funding is announced
- 30M+ companies, 190M+ people, refreshed in real-time

### CB Insights — The Market Intelligence Platform

- **Price:** $50,000+/yr (enterprise only)
- **Data:** Technology market intelligence — patents, funding patterns, hiring trends, news sentiment.
- **Strengths:** Trend identification, sector landscaping, expert collections. Used by corporate strategy teams.
- **Weakness:** Enterprise-only pricing. Oriented toward corporate innovation teams, not individual investors.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** CB Insights serves Fortune 500 corporate dev teams. Deep Thesis serves angels and emerging managers. Almost zero customer overlap. **Different market entirely.**

### Dealum — Deal Flow Management

- **Price:** Not publicly listed (likely low hundreds/mo)
- **Data:** Not a data platform — it's a deal flow pipeline tool.
- **Strengths:** Syndication, co-investment, group portfolio management. Strong in European angel networks.
- **Weakness:** No proprietary company data, no AI analysis, no scoring.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** Dealum manages your deal process. Deep Thesis generates the intelligence that informs decisions within that process. **Complementary — a Dealum user could use Deep Thesis for analysis.**

### Landscape.vc — AI Startup Discovery

- **Price:** Starts at $30/mo trial, monthly per-seat subscription
- **Data:** AI-powered discovery with custom signal feeds, daily data refreshes.
- **Strengths:** Affordable. Custom signal feeds matching investment thesis. Workflow automations.
- **Weakness:** Focused on discovery, not analysis or scoring.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** Similar price range. Landscape helps you find startups; Deep Thesis helps you evaluate them. **Potential integration partner or acquisition target.**

### Signal by NFX — Free Matching

- **Price:** Free
- **Data:** Investor-founder matching network.
- **Strengths:** Free. Network effects. Intro-path finding.
- **Weakness:** Not a data or analysis tool — it's a matching marketplace.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** Not competitive. Signal helps founders find investors. Deep Thesis helps investors evaluate companies.

### Visible.vc — Portfolio Monitoring

- **Price:** $449/mo (investor plan)
- **Data:** Automates portfolio KPI collection from 540+ funds.
- **Strengths:** LP reporting, portfolio data collection, Stripe/QuickBooks integrations.
- **Weakness:** Post-investment tool — no deal sourcing or pre-investment analysis.
- **vs. Deep Thesis:** Visible is post-investment (monitor your portfolio). Deep Thesis is pre-investment (should you invest?). **No overlap.**

## Deep Thesis Positioning

### What's Unique

1. **AI Analysis at angel prices** — 8-agent startup analysis, scored investment memos, and a conversational VC quant agent for $20/mo. Nobody else offers AI-driven diligence below $5K/yr.

2. **Investor Rankings** — No other platform publicly scores and ranks investors across 7 dimensions. This is a new category. The marketing play ("you've been ranked — come see your profile") is a growth channel none of the competitors have.

3. **Buy-side transaction data** — 1,000+ closed VC transactions with pricing/terms. Most platforms track announced rounds but not actual buy-side deal terms. This is the PitchBook-level data at 100x less cost.

4. **Secondaries market data** — Virtually no one at this price point surfaces secondary market pricing data on venture-backed companies.

### Where Deep Thesis Is Weaker

| Gap | Who does it better |
|-----|-------------------|
| Data breadth (6M vs 2.8K companies) | PitchBook, Crunchbase, Harmonic |
| Stealth/pre-seed sourcing | Harmonic (30M+ companies) |
| CRM / relationship tracking | Affinity, Dealum |
| Portfolio monitoring / LP reporting | Visible.vc, Edda |
| Enterprise integrations (Excel, Salesforce) | PitchBook |

### The Strategic Bet

Deep Thesis wins by being the "good enough" data platform that's 50-100x cheaper with AI analysis that the incumbents don't have. The target customer can't justify PitchBook but needs more than Crunchbase — and wants AI to do the analysis they can't afford to hire an analyst for.
