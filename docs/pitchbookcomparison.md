# Deep Thesis vs PitchBook — Feature Gap Analysis

## What Deep Thesis Currently Has
- Company name, description, tagline, logo
- Stage, location, founded date
- Founders (name, title, LinkedIn)
- Funding rounds (round name, amount, date, lead investor)
- Total funding, employee count
- Industries, competitors, tech stack, key metrics
- Social links (LinkedIn, Twitter, Crunchbase, website)
- AI-generated analysis (thesis, risks, verdict, dimension scores)
- Media coverage
- Score history/tracking

## What PitchBook Has That We're Missing

### Company Data
- **Valuation** — pre-money and post-money per round
- **Revenue / ARR** — estimated or reported revenue ranges
- **Business model type** — SaaS, marketplace, hardware, etc. (structured, not just in description)
- **Year founded** vs just "founded date"
- **Status** — active, acquired, IPO, defunct

### Investors (big gap)
- **All investors per round**, not just lead — PitchBook lists every participant
- **Investor profiles** — separate pages for firms with their portfolio, fund size, investment thesis
- **Board members and advisors**

### People
- **Full management team**, not just founders — CTO, CFO, VP Sales, etc.
- **Prior experience** — previous companies, exits, education
- **Board seats held**

### Deals & Transactions
- **Deal multiples** — revenue multiples, valuation benchmarks
- **Debt financing** — not just equity rounds
- **M&A activity** — acquisitions made by the company
- **Exit details** — IPO pricing, acquisition terms

### Market Context
- **Comparable companies** — structured comps with side-by-side metrics
- **Market size estimates** — TAM/SAM/SOM
- **Industry reports and segments**

### Financials
- **Revenue estimates**
- **Growth rate**
- **Burn rate / runway**
- **Unit economics** if available

## Priorities

The biggest gaps that would make Deep Thesis feel like a real data product:

1. **All investors per round** (not just lead) — table stakes for deal data
2. **Valuation per round** — the #1 thing people look up on PitchBook
3. **Company status** (active/acquired/IPO/dead) — critical for tracking outcomes
4. **Revenue estimates** — even ranges ("$1M-$10M ARR") add huge value
5. **Full investor profiles** — makes the platform a network graph, not just a list
6. **Comparable companies** as structured data, not just a text blob in "competitors"
